Interview with grandparents

The survival of wild animals depends on how well a community lives in harmony with them. It is a traditional knowledge or culture that should be passed from generation to generation. If we think that our grandparents of the previous generation lived in harmony with nature without conflict, then it becomes part of our duty to know whether this opinion is true about our community and if there are conservation practices, to follow them.

This activity has been designed to respect the experience-based knowledge base of the elderly souls, to interview them and record their experiences in today's mechanical life, where the occasions to listen to their incredible stories are rare, and to respond to their many life experiences.

Health i.e. they need a safe habitat for them to live comfortably and reproduce.Is their habitat safe now? Today's children need to understand the quality of habitats, the pressure on habitats, the problems they are facing, the destruction of various plants that depend on them for food, habitats, the increasing human interference in the different ecosystems they live in, and they need to understand the impact of these problems on their parents.

Group Size: Children of the whole class/school – can do this alone or in teams of twos and threes
Duration: About an hour of interview and two hours of discussion, interaction in the school room
Materials: Interview form, sheets, pen etc.
Objective: To make students aware of conservation as a culture and part of life and to adopt some good points.
Procedure:Make some preparations a day before the activity. Prepare some sample questions. Have some preparatory conversation, tell the children the manners to be followed while interviewing the elder who speaks respectfully.
Pattern of Questions – These questions should not be asked in the same manner. This is just a sample. Appropriate to your town, your language, questions that are understandable to elders 1. When you were a child, did you see a wild boar around this town? If so, how many have you seen? (Enter the approximate age if they are younger) 2. Did your parents see Wild Sins when they were young? 3. What would you do if you saw wild boar in or around your town? Running scared? Are you stoned? Leaving them alone? 4. Remember any superstitions about these wild sins? For example, wild boars are bad omens, diseases are cured by their blood, etc. 5. Do you remember any story / poem about forest sins? 6. Has the number of wild boars decreased or increased in recent years? Why? 7. Have you noticed wild boar hunting? them
